Understanding Your Triceps

The triceps brachii is a three-headed muscle located on the back of the upper arm. Despite being often overlooked in favor of the biceps, the triceps actually makes up about two-thirds of your upper arm mass and is crucial for arm strength and aesthetics.

Effective triceps training targets all three heads:

  • Long Head - The largest head, runs along the back of the arm
  • Lateral (Outer) Head - Located on the outside of the arm, gives width to the triceps
  • Medial (Inner) Head - The smallest head, located on the inner side of the arm

Well-developed triceps not only create impressive arm definition but also support performance in pushing exercises like bench press, overhead press, and push-ups.

Triceps Training Tips

Form & Technique

  • Keep your elbows close to your body during most triceps exercises
  • Avoid flaring your elbows outward, which shifts focus away from the triceps
  • Focus on full extension at the end of each repetition
  • Minimize shoulder movement during isolation exercises
  • Concentrate on feeling the triceps working throughout the movement

Programming Guidelines

  • Train triceps 1-2 times per week, allowing 48-72 hours for recovery
  • Start with compound movements, then move to isolation exercises
  • For mass: 8-10 reps, for definition: 12-15 reps
  • Include exercises that target all three heads for complete development
  • Pair triceps with chest or shoulders (push muscles) in your training split

Injury Prevention

  • Warm up thoroughly with light weights or resistance bands
  • Never lock out your elbows forcefully at the top of extensions
  • Maintain control throughout the negative (eccentric) phase
  • If experiencing elbow pain, check your form and consider reducing weight
  • Balance triceps work with biceps training for joint health

Nutrition Support

  • Consume adequate protein (1.6-2.2g per kg of bodyweight)
  • Ensure sufficient carbohydrates for energy during intense training
  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after workouts
  • Consider a protein/carb shake within 30 minutes post-workout
  • For growth, maintain a slight caloric surplus; for definition, a moderate deficit

Common Triceps Training Mistakes

Excessive Elbow Flare

Allowing elbows to flare out during movements shifts focus from the triceps to the shoulders.

Correct Approach:

Keep elbows tucked in close to your body, especially during pushdowns and close-grip presses.

Incomplete Range of Motion

Not fully extending the arms or not lowering the weight enough reduces triceps development.

Correct Approach:

Work through a complete range of motion, from a full stretch to a complete contraction.

Neglecting All Three Heads

Focusing only on certain exercises can lead to imbalanced triceps development.

Correct Approach:

Include a variety of exercises that target all three heads of the triceps for complete development.

Frequently Asked Questions

For optimal growth, train triceps 1-2 times per week with at least 48-72 hours of recovery between sessions. Since triceps are involved in many pushing movements, they're often trained on chest or shoulder days in a split routine.

Triceps are heavily involved as secondary muscles in most chest pressing movements. If you train chest before triceps, your triceps are already partially fatigued, which can reduce their ability to handle direct triceps work later. Consider either training triceps before chest, or on separate days, or ensuring adequate recovery between chest and triceps training.

Compound exercises like close-grip bench press, weighted dips, and skull crushers are most effective for building triceps mass. These exercises allow you to handle heavier loads, creating greater mechanical tension, which is a primary driver of muscle growth. Follow these compound movements with isolation exercises like pushdowns and overhead extensions to complete your workout.

"Bat wings" are primarily a result of excess body fat combined with underdeveloped triceps. A two-pronged approach is necessary: 1) Reduce overall body fat through proper nutrition and a caloric deficit, as spot reduction is not possible; 2) Build triceps muscle through regular strength training focusing on all three heads. Overhead triceps extensions and dips are particularly effective for targeting the long head, which makes up most of the back of the arm.

Elbow pain during triceps training can be caused by several factors: improper form, overtraining, using too much weight, or medical conditions like tendonitis. To prevent elbow pain, ensure proper warm-up, use controlled movements, avoid locking out your elbows forcefully, and use appropriate weight. If pain persists, reduce weight and volume, check your form, and consider consulting with a healthcare professional if the issue continues.
Close-Grip Bench Press

Target Muscles: All three triceps heads, chest (secondary), shoulders (secondary)

Equipment: Barbell, bench

Difficulty: Beginner

Instructions
  1. Lie on a flat bench with your feet firmly on the ground.
  2. Grip the barbell with hands shoulder-width apart or slightly closer.
  3. Unrack the barbell and hold it directly above your chest with arms fully extended.
  4. Lower the bar in a controlled manner to your lower chest.
  5. Press the bar back up by extending your elbows, focusing on using your triceps.
Key Form Points
  • Keep your elbows tucked close to your body
  • Avoid flaring your elbows outward
  • Maintain a stable position on the bench
  • Keep wrists straight throughout the movement
    • Variations
    • Smith machine close-grip bench press
    • Close-grip dumbbell press
    • Incline close-grip bench press
Triceps Pushdown

Target Muscles: Lateral and medial heads of the triceps

Equipment: Cable machine with straight bar or rope attachment

Difficulty: Beginner

Instructions
  1. Stand facing a cable machine with a straight bar or rope attachment connected to the high pulley.
  2. Grip the attachment with hands shoulder-width apart, palms facing down.
  3. Keep your elbows close to your sides and at approximately 90 degrees.
  4. Push the attachment down by extending your elbows until your arms are straight.
  5. Pause briefly at the bottom, then slowly return to the starting position.
Key Form Points
  • Keep your upper arms stationary throughout
  • Maintain a slight forward lean from the hips
  • Focus on squeezing the triceps at full extension
  • Control the weight during the eccentric (upward) phase
Variations
  • Rope pushdown (allows for greater rotation)
  • V-bar pushdown (neutral grip position)
  • Single-arm pushdown (addresses muscle imbalances)
